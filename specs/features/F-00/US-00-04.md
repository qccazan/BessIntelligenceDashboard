# US-00-04 · Battery Historical Time Series (D-03)

## User Story

**As a** developer building the PoC data layer, **I want to** provide per-asset time series of power output and state of charge at 15-minute resolution over the previous 24 hours, **so that** the Last 24 Hours Replay card can render power bars, SoC curves, and daily energy summary chips.

## Description

Battery Historical Time Series contains per-asset time series of power output and state of charge at 15-minute resolution over the previous 24 hours. 96 data points per asset. This is the primary dataset for the Last 24 Hours Replay card and for calculating daily energy throughput. Stored in `battery_history_24h.json` as an object keyed by asset ID, each value an array of 96 readings.

Each reading contains: timestamp (start of the 15-minute interval), average power during the interval (`power_kw`, positive = charging), and state of charge at the end of the interval (`soc_pct`).

Derived fields computed from the series (not stored): Total charged (kWh) = sum of max(power_kw, 0) × 0.25; Total discharged (kWh) = sum of max(-power_kw, 0) × 0.25; Net cycles = (charged + discharged) ÷ 2 ÷ capacity_kwh.

## Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1**: The dataset contains entries for all 12 assets, keyed by asset ID matching D-01.
- **AC-2**: Each asset has exactly 96 time-series readings (24 hours × 4 per hour).
- **AC-3**: Each reading contains: `timestamp`, `power_kw`, and `soc_pct`.
- **AC-4**: Timestamps are spaced exactly 15 minutes apart and span a continuous 24-hour period.
- **AC-5**: The `soc_pct` value is between 0 and 100 (inclusive) at every interval.
- **AC-6**: The last interval's `soc_pct` for each asset equals the corresponding `soc_pct` in D-02 (SoC continuity rule).
- **AC-7**: SoC values increase during charging intervals (positive power_kw) and decrease during discharging intervals (negative power_kw), proportional to power and capacity.
- **AC-8**: All timestamps use UTC-offset format (Europe/Amsterdam, CET/CEST).
- **AC-9**: No gaps, duplicate timestamps, or out-of-order entries exist in any asset's time series.
