# US-06-05 · Per-Battery Assignment

## User Story

**As a** dispatch engine mapping portfolio-level decisions to individual assets, **I want to** assign Charge, Discharge, or Hold actions to each battery based on eligibility rules and stagger start times to reduce grid coincidence, **so that** each asset receives appropriate instructions and the grid impact is minimised.

## Description

Eligibility rules determine per-battery assignment: fault assets → Hold ("Fault — held offline"), SoH < 75% → Hold ("SoH below dispatch threshold"), SoC > charge_ceiling − 5% → Discharge at peak or Hold ("High SoC — dispatch at peak"), SoC < discharge_floor + 10% → Charge with delayed start +30 min ("Low SoC — delayed start"), negative net_benefit → Hold ("Marginal asset — held to protect longevity"), all others → Charge at optimal window.

Start times are staggered: eligible assets sorted by available_to_charge_kwh descending, grouped in threes, each group offset by 15 minutes. This reduces coincident demand from ~5.6 MW to ~1.9 MW per slot. Discharge staggering follows the same logic in reverse.

## Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1**: Fault-mode assets (D-02 mode = "fault") are assigned Hold with reason "Fault — held offline".
- **AC-2**: Assets with SoH < 75% are assigned Hold with reason "SoH below dispatch threshold".
- **AC-3**: Assets with SoC > charge_ceiling − 5% (i.e. > 85%) are assigned Discharge or Hold with reason "High SoC — dispatch at peak".
- **AC-4**: Assets with SoC < discharge_floor + 10% (i.e. < 25%) are assigned Charge with delayed start (+30 min) and reason "Low SoC — delayed start".
- **AC-5**: Assets with negative net_benefit are assigned Hold with reason "Marginal asset — held to protect longevity".
- **AC-6**: All remaining eligible assets are assigned Charge at the optimal window.
- **AC-7**: Start times are staggered in groups of 3, offset by 15 minutes.
- **AC-8**: Hold assets have window_start and window_end set to "—".
- **AC-9**: Delayed-start assets share the same window_end as the portfolio window.
