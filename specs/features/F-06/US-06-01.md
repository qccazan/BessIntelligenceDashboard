# US-06-01 · Price Signal Processing

## User Story

**As a** dispatch engine processing market data, **I want to** load the 24-hour EPEX SPOT NL price array, enumerate candidate charge/discharge windows, apply minimum spread filtering, and adjust prices for solar irradiance, **so that** only viable and weather-corrected window pairs are passed to the optimisation step.

## Description

The engine loads the 24-hour EPEX SPOT NL day-ahead price array from D-04 and identifies the optimal windows for charging (buy cheap) and discharging (sell expensive). This involves four sub-steps:

**Step 1a — Candidate Window Enumeration:** For each possible contiguous block of 1–4 hours, compute the average price. Windows shorter than 1 hour or longer than 4 hours are excluded. Discharge windows must start ≥ 1 hour after the charge window ends (rest constraint).

**Step 1b — Minimum Spread Filter:** A window pair is only eligible if gross_spread ≥ 40 EUR/MWh, covering round-trip efficiency losses (~5% at 95% charge + 95% discharge efficiency, plus ~2 EUR/MWh TenneT imbalance settlement buffer).

**Step 1c — Solar Irradiance Price Adjustment:** On sunny days, actual intraday prices during peak solar hours (09:00–15:00) deviate downward 15–35%. The engine applies: `solar_price_adjustment = avg_portfolio_ghi × 0.00055 × raw_price_h`, with a floor of 5 EUR/MWh. All subsequent scoring uses weather-corrected prices.

**Step 1d — Solar Duck Window Detection:** On high-GHI days (avg_portfolio_ghi > 350 W/m² between 09:00–15:00), a secondary midday charge window ("solar duck") is added if the weather-corrected price is below the 24h median and the spread vs. evening discharge clears 40 EUR/MWh.

## Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1**: The engine loads exactly 24 hourly price entries from D-04.
- **AC-2**: Candidate windows are enumerated for all contiguous blocks of 1–4 hours within the 24-hour horizon.
- **AC-3**: Discharge windows must start at least 1 hour after the charge window ends.
- **AC-4**: Window pairs with gross_spread < 40 EUR/MWh are filtered out.
- **AC-5**: Solar price adjustment is applied: `adjustment = avg_portfolio_ghi × 0.00055 × raw_price`, with weather-corrected price floored at 5 EUR/MWh.
- **AC-6**: A solar duck window is added when avg_portfolio_ghi > 350 W/m² during 09:00–15:00, the weather-corrected price is below the 24h median, and the spread vs. discharge window clears 40 EUR/MWh.
- **AC-7**: On cloudy days (GHI ≤ 350 W/m²), the solar duck window is not added.
- **AC-8**: All subsequent window scoring uses weather-corrected prices, not raw prices.
